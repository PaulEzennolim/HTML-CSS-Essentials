Accessibility

Visual disabilities

I made sure to make my text large and I used colours that contrast well. 8% of the world suffer from colour blindness so making my text readable was a key aspect of my accessibility approach.

I also included alternative text for all my images

Navigation was also made easy to assist with visual disabilities. I put things like headers and links places that are easy to see.

Auditory disabilities

Users might not be able to hear sounds or listen to videos. I made interactions only pictorial because of this.

Cognitive disabilities

Cognitive disabilities affect users in that they may struggle with complex layouts or navigation menus. To help with this i made the layout of my website simple, so that all people can navigate around it easily.

Speech disabilities

Users might not be able to communicate with organisations when on the website there is just a telephone number. So to help with this i provided alternative ways to contact the committee, such as email/form/chat etc.

Accessibility handled by other software

Screen readers: Software like Jaws, NVDA and voiceover read aloud the text on screen. This will help users with visual disabilities to navigate the content

Speech recognition software: Windows speech recognition allows users to contol a computer using voice commands, which may help people who have limited mobility
